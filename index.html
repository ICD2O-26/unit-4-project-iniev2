What is an automaton?

An automaton is a relatively self-operating/controlling mechanism designed to follow a sequence of predetermined instructions. In the past, automatons (c. 1800s) were used for entertainment, moving throughout exhibits before retiring when they became too brittle. Very old automatons, however, were used in a more practical manner.


Aside from old ways of use, automatons are still used in modern day. The coocoo clock is an automaton, as well as the well-known toy of the monkey bashing the cymbals together (and any other self-moving wind up toy).


This is Maillardet's automaton. It is a personal favorite of mine. It travelled through exhibitions from 1807-1837. Almost 100 years later, it was given to The Franklin Institute by the decendants of the man who built it, John Penn Brock. The pieces appeared to have been in a fire. It was restored in 2007. It has the ability to write poems and draw pictures.


Throughout many, many years, automatons have changed and evolved their mechanisms a lot. In fact, there is proof that automatons have existed since ancient times! At some point, automata went from being used for practical purposes to being used for entertainment (while some sectors kept making useful automata), then, it went back to being mostly practical automatons. Here is an example of an ancient automaton.


This is the Antikythera mechanism. It is an automaton from 2nd century BC and was discovered in the early 1900s. It was used to predict eclipses and astronomical positions years in advance. The Antikythera mechanism is the earliest form of the analog computer.



Automatons contributed and continue to contribute to the evolving of modern technology as a whole, being based around self-operation. Self-operation is used widely across so many different types of machines, these complex mechanisms deriving from automatons of our past. However, automatons run risks of breaking after a long time. This does not result in destruction or injury.
Older automatons caused no tragedies, but newer ones like self-driving cars are known to be dangerous. Be careful!


Automatons of the past were used in numerous ways, but the most common time to see them was in the 1600s-1800s. During that time, they were most often used as entertainment. Though other forms of automata were also used (such as the clock). Nowadays, automatons are most commonly used very practically, as tools. Some examples of this would be the sewing machine, the car wash, and the record player. Although, there are many more machines in the world than there were 200 years ago, so we have more statistics to go off of. A lot of mass-produced children's toys can be considered automatons as well.
