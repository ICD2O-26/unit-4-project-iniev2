Automatons of the past and present.

By Izzy!


What is an automaton?

An automaton is a relatively self-operating/controlling mechanism designed to follow a sequence of predetermined instructions. In the past, automatons (c. 1800s) were used for entertainment, moving throughout exhibits before retiring when they became too brittle. Very old automatons, however, were used in a more practical manner. The antikythera mechanism (200-80BC) is an ancient device that could predict astronomical positions. It is the oldest known form of the analogue computer.

Aside from old ways of use, automatons are still used in modern day. The coocoo clock is an automaton, as well as the well-known toy of the monkey bashing the cymbals together (and any other self-moving wind up toy).


This is Maillardet's automaton. It is a personal favorite of mine. It travelled through exhibitions from 1807-1837. Almost 100 years later, it was given to The Franklin Institute by the decendants of the man who built it, John Penn Brock. The pieces appeared to have been in a fire. It was restored in 2007. It has the ability to write poems and draw pictures.




Automatons contributed and continue to contribute to the evolving of modern technology as a whole, being based around self-operation. Self-operation is used widely across so many different types of machines, these complex mechanisms deriving from automatons of our past.

However, automatons run risks of breaking after a long time. This does not result in destruction or injury.

Older automatons caused no tragedies, but newer ones like self-driving cars are known to be dangerous. Be careful!
